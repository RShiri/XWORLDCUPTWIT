Expected Goals · Efficiency

xG Efficiency Lab

Do teams score as much as the quality of their chances says they should? These charts compare expected goals (xG) with what actually happened.

New here? What is xG?

Expected goals (xG) rates every shot by how likely it was to be scored, from 0 to 1. Add up a team's shots and you get how many goals an average finisher would have scored from those chances.

We built the model ourselves. Every shot is scored by a blend of logistic regression and gradient boosting, trained on ~78,000 real shots. Expected assists (xA) works the same way for passes, trained on 2.4 million real passes.

Did chances turn into goals?

Every dot is one team's performance in one match: expected (across →) vs actual (up ↑). Above the dashed line = clinical finishing; below = chances wasted.

How closely do goals follow xG?

If xG were perfect, goals would track it exactly. This measures how strong that link is.
